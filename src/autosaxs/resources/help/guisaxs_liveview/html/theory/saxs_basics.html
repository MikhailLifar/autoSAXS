SAXS basics (minimal)

Small-angle X-ray scattering (SAXS) measures intensity vs scattering vector q. 2D detector images are integrated to 1D I(q) curves, then interpreted with GNOM (p(r)), ab initio models (DAMMIF), geometric bodies (BODIES), or polydisperse / mixture tools.

guisaxs-liveview automates that chain for streaming TIFFs; scientific choices remain in the wizards.
